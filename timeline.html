1869-1941

    1869 - The Cincinnati Red Stocking finish with a 57-0 record.
    1881 - The Cincinnati Reds are founded.
    1882 - The Reds clinch the American Association pennant which is the first championship won by a proffesional sports team in Cincinnati.
    1891 - The first Opening Day parade is organized.
    1902 - The "Palace of the Fans" hosts it's first game.
    1912 - Redland Field (later known as Crosley Field) hosts its first game.
    1919 - The Reds clinch the first National pennant in franchise history.
    1919 - The Reds win the franchise's first National League pennant and go on to win their first World Series as well.
    1935 - The first name game in Major League history is played at Crosley Field.
    1938 - Johhny Vander Meer pitches a no-hitter against the Boston Braves.
    1938 - Johhny Vander Meer pitches his second straight no-hitter against against the dodgers and remains the only pitcher in Major League baseball to do so.
    1938 - Crosley Field hosts the All-Star game as the NL wins, 4-1.
    1939 - The Reds clinch the franchise's second National League pennant but get swept by the Yankees in the World Series.
    1941 - The Reds win their second World Series.

1944-1990

    1944 - Pitcher Joe Nuxhall becomes the youngest player in the 20th century to appear in a Major League game at the age of 15.
    1947 - Ewell Blackwell sets the NL record for righthanders and a club record winning his 16th consecutive start.
    1949 - Walker Cooper sets a club record of 10 rbi's in a game.
    1958 - Bucky Walters, Ernie Lombardi, Paul Derringer, Johhny Vander Meer, Frank Mccormick are the first inductees into the Reds Hall of Fame.
    1970 - The last game is played at Crosley Field.
    1970 - Riverfront Stadium opens and 51,050 fans attend it's first game.
    1970 - Riverfront Stadium hosts the All-Star game as the NL wins 5-4.
    1970 - The Reds complete a sweep of the Pirates to win the first National League Championship series in history.
    1971 - One of the most significant trades in Reds history occurs as the Reds send Lee May, Tommy Helms, and Jimmy Stewart to the Astros for Joe Morgan, Jack Billingham, Cesar Geronimo, Ed Armbrister, and Dennis Menke.
    1972 - The Reds come back down 2 to beat the Pirates in the NLDS. The Reds then get swept by the Yankees in the World Series.
    1975 - The Reds win their first World Series in 35 years after defeat the Boston Red Sox 4-3 in game 7.
    1976 - The Reds win their second straight World Series by sweeping the Yankees.
    1977 - George Foster hits his club record 52nd home run against the Padres.
    1978 - Pete Rose extend his hitting streak to 44 consecutive games which puts him behind Joe Dimaggio record of 56.
    1983 - Johhny Bench records his 389th homer which was the last in his career.
    1984 - Pete Rose debuts as the Reds player-manager as they defeat the Cubs 6-4.
    1985 - Pete Rose becomes baseball's all time hits leader with career hit number 4,192.
    1987 - Eric Davis becomes the first player in franchis history to hit 30 home runs and steal 30 bases in a season.
    1988 - The All-Star game returns to Riverfront Stadium as the AL defeats the NL 2-1.
    1988 - Tom Browning becomes the first pitcher in Reds history to throw a perfect game.
    1989 - Pete Rose is given a lifetime ban from baseball for conduct related to gambling.
    1990 - The Reds sweep the heavily favored Oakland A's in the World Series.

1995-2025

    1995 - Barry Larkin wins the National League MVP.
    1996 - Riverfront Stadium is renamed Cynergy Field.
    1997 - Barry Larkin becomes the first shortstop in MLB history to hit 30 home runs and steal 30 bases in a season.
    1999 - The Reds finish with an impressive 96-66 record but fall to the Mets in the sixth 1-game playoff in MLB history.
    2000 - Ken Griffey Jr. signs with the Reds.
    2002 - Cynergy Field is torn down.
    2003 - The Reds play their first game at Great American Ball Park and 42,343 attent the game.
    2004 - Ken Griffey Jr. hits his 500th career home run on Fathers Day in front of his father.
    2004 - Danny Graves became the club's all-time saves leader.
    2006 - Aaron Harang leads the NL with 216 srikeouts and six complete games and tied for the league lead in wins with 16.
    2007 - Brandon Phillips emerges as one of the best all-around players in baseball. Phillips becomes the second second baseman in league history to reach the 30/30 mark.
    2008 - Dusty Baker begins his first season as Reds manager.
    2008 - Ken Griffey Jr. hits his 600th career home run.
    2008 - Ken Griffey Jr. and Adam Dunn are traded.
    2008 - Edison Volquez makes the All-Star game and finishes the season with 17 wins.
    2009 - Joey Votto leads the team with a .322 average and 25 home runs.
    2009 - Bronson Arroyo finishes the season with 15 wins.
    2010 - The Reds have their first winning season since 2000 and reach the playoffs after winning the NL Central division.
    2010 - Bronson Arroyo tallies a career high of 17 wins.
    2010 - The Reds run ends early after getting swept by the Phillies in the NLDS.
    2012 - Joey Votto and Brandon Phillips are signed to long term contracts.
    2012 - Homer Bailey pitches a no-hitter against the Pirates.
    2012 - The Reds win 97 games and win the NL Central.
    2012 - The Reds season ends in crushing fashion after losing the NLDS to the eventual World Series winning Giants.
    2013 - Homer Bailey throws his second career no-hitter against the Giants.
    2013 - The Reds squeak into the playoffs but lose in the Wild Card to the Pirates.
    2013 - Dusty Baker is fired.
    2015 - Todd Frazier wins the Home Run Derby at the All-Star game hosted by the Reds at Great American Ballpark.
    2015 - The Reds trade away key players to begin a rebuild.
    2016 - Jay Bruce is traded to the Mets.
    2017 - Zack Cozart makes the All-Star game for the first time in his career and Tucker Barnhart wins his first Gold Glove award.
    2017 - Joey Votto has the best season of his career producing at least 26 homers and 100 RBIs while hitting at least .300 and an OBP of .400 and a slugging percentage of .500.
    2018 - Bryan Price is Fired.
    2019 - The Reds hire David Bell.
    2020 - Trevor Bauer becomes the first Cy Young Award winner in Reds history.
    2020 - The Reds make the extended playoffs but are swept by the Braves in 2 games.
    2021 - Nick Castellanos and Jesse Winker make the All-Star game.
    2021 - Joey Votto hits 36 home runs at 37 years old.
    2022 - The Reds begin another rebuild.
    2023 - Elly De La Cruz makes his debut.
    2023 - Joey Votto plays in his final Major League game.
    2023 - The Reds finish with a surprising 82-80 season with young prospects Elly De La Cruz, Matt McLain, Christian Encarnacion Strand and Andrew Abbott.
    2024 - Elly De La Cruz and Hunter Greene make the All-Star game for the first time in their careers.
    2024 - David Bell is fired.
    2025 - The Reds hire Terry Francona.
    2025 - The Reds make the playoffs but get swept by the Dodgers in the Wild Card.
