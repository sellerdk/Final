Throughout history the Cincinnati Franchise has had plenty of success. While the Reds have a rich championship history it had been overshadowed by the teams lack of success for the last 35 years.

1919

In 1919 the Cincinnati Reds would win their first World Series. While this was a big moment for the Reds this World Series was quite controversial. This World Series was controversial because of the Black Sox Scandal where Multiple players on the White Sox were fixing games from the start of the series. Even with this it would take the Reds eight games to clinch the series

1940

In 1940 the Reds were recovering from being swept by the Yankees in the 1939 World Series. This would not stop the Reds as they would go on to defeat the Detriot Tigers in seven games. In game one the Tigers had the Reds number as they would run Reds pitcher Paul Derringer out of the game early and win game 1 7-2. The Reds would get their revenge and win the next game 5-3 behind Jimmy Ripple's two-run homer. The series would go back and forth after this but the Reds would win the final game.

1975 and 1976

In 1975 and 1976 the Reds would win their back to back World Series in franchise history behind the Big Red Machine. In 1975 the Reds would defeat the Boston Red Sox in 7 games thanks to great performances from Pete Rose. In 1976 the Reds would win the World Series with ease sweeping the New York Yankees.

1990

In 1990 the Oakland A's were heavily favored to win as they were seen as the more dominant team. This did not faze the Reds as they would sweep the A's to many peoples surprise. Jose Rijo and Chris Sabo helped lead the Reds to their fifth world series championship.
